# **PHASE 1: FOUNDATION BUILD**

## **WEEKS 1-12 (MAY 4 - JULY 26, 2026)**

### **Duration: 12 weeks**

No heroics. No complicated structure. Just get out the door 4 days a week and build the engine.

---

## **PRIMARY GOALS:**

1. ✅ Build aerobic base - easy pace improves from ~6:40/km to ~6:00/km naturally
2. ✅ Extend Sunday long run from 5km → 16km
3. ✅ Establish Thursday gym habit (Nordic curls are your hamstring insurance)
4. ✅ Lose 3-4kg without trying (it happens when you train consistently)
5. ✅ Build the 4-day habit - no missed weeks

---

## **TRAINING PHILOSOPHY:**

- **Easy runs must be EASY** - if you can't hold a conversation, slow down. No ego.
- **Long run is sacred** - Sunday morning, protect it
- **Thursday gym is injury prevention** - one hamstring pull ends your race season
- **Consistency beats intensity at this stage** - 12 boring weeks builds a real base
- **You are building an engine right now, not racing**

### **THURSDAY: GYM SESSION (before 17:30)**

**Get there by 16:30 at the latest. Session is 45-50min.**

This is injury prevention, not bodybuilding. Keep weights moderate and controlled.

---

**1. Nordic Curls — THE CRITICAL EXERCISE**

You were a sprinter. Your hamstrings are both your greatest asset and your biggest injury risk. Nordic curls will keep you running when others get hurt.

| Week | Sets x Reps |
|------|------------|
| 1-2 | 3 x 4 (5sec lower, very controlled) |
| 3-4 | 3 x 5 |
| 5-6 | 3 x 6 |
| 7-8 | 3 x 7 |
| 9-10 | 3 x 8 |
| 11-12 | 3 x 9 |

**How to do them:**
- Kneel on a mat, anchor feet under a bench or have someone hold them
- Slowly lower your body forward, controlling entirely with your hamstrings (5 seconds down)
- Catch yourself with your hands at the bottom
- Push back up to start position
- Rest 90 seconds between sets
- **Never skip these**

---

**2. Single-Leg Romanian Deadlifts**
- Bodyweight or light dumbbell (5-10kg)
- 3 sets x 8 reps per leg
- Hinge at the hip, feel the hamstring stretch
- Rest 60sec

**3. Glute Bridges**
- 3 sets x 15 reps
- Hold top 2 seconds, squeeze glutes hard
- Rest 60sec

**4. Plank**
- 3 sets x 30-40sec
- Build to 50sec by Week 12
- Rest 45sec

**5. Dead Bugs**
- 3 sets x 10 reps (5 each side)
- Keep your lower back flat on the floor the entire time

**Optional if time allows:**
- Pull-ups or lat pulldown: 3 x 6-8
- Push-ups: 3 x 12

**Total gym time: 40-50min. Get in, work hard, get out.**

### **SUNDAY: LONG RUN (morning)**

**🔥 THE MOST IMPORTANT SESSION OF THE WEEK 🔥**

This is where your half marathon is built. Every Sunday long run is a deposit into your race day account.

**Pre-run routine (every Sunday, no exceptions):**
- Wake up, bathroom
- Light breakfast 60-90min before:
  - 2 slices toast + honey + banana, OR
  - Oats (50g) + banana + honey
- 500ml water
- 5min easy walk before starting to run

| Week | Distance | Pace | Notes |
|------|----------|------|-------|
| 1 | 5km | 6:30-7:00/km | Get moving, shake the rust off |
| 2 | 6km | 6:30-7:00/km | |
| 3 | 7km | 6:20-6:50/km | |
| 4 | 8km | 6:20-6:50/km | |
| 5 | 9km | 6:10-6:40/km | First time near 10km territory |
| 6 | 10km | 6:10-6:40/km | Double digits |
| 7 | 11km | 6:00-6:30/km | |
| 8 | 12km | 6:00-6:30/km | |
| 9 | 13km | 5:55-6:20/km | |
| 10 | 14km | 5:50-6:15/km | |
| 11 | 15km | 5:45-6:10/km | |
| 12 | 16km | 5:45-6:10/km | You've run 16km. The race is 21. |

**Rules for the long run:**
- Start slow. First km should feel embarrassingly easy.
- Walk breaks allowed in early weeks if needed - just keep moving
- Carry water for anything 10km+
- Don't race it. This is aerobic base building, not a time trial.
- Finish feeling like you could've done 1-2km more

**Post-run protocol:**
- 5min walk immediately (don't stop suddenly)
- 500ml water straight away
- Eat within 30min (banana + something with protein - eggs, chicken, milk)
- Stretch 10min: hamstrings, quads, calves, hip flexors (hold 30-60sec each)
- Foam roll if you have access (quads, calves, IT band)

## **EXAM PERIODS:**

Physics and CompSci exams are not negotiable. The plan bends around them.

**If exams hit during this phase:**
- Drop to 3 sessions that week (cut Monday or Wednesday, keep Sunday long run + Thursday gym)
- Reduce distances by 30-40%
- Easy pace only, nothing hard
- You will NOT lose your base in one exam week
- Come back the following week and continue where you left off

**The rule:** Never sacrifice exams for training. But also don't use exams as an excuse to miss 3 weeks.
