### **WEDNESDAY: HARD WORKOUT DAY 🔥**

This is where fitness is built. Alternate between two workout types each week.

---

**WARM-UP (always, every session - non-negotiable):**
- 2km easy jog
- Leg swings: 10 forward/back each leg
- High knees: 20m x 2
- Butt kicks: 20m x 2
- A-skips: 20m x 2 (drive knee up, push off ball of foot)
- B-skips: 20m x 2 (A-skip + extend leg forward)
- 4 x 100m strides @ 70% effort

*The A-skips and B-skips matter for you specifically. You were a sprinter. These drills reconnect your fast-twitch mechanics with your distance running.*

**COOL-DOWN (always):**
- 2km easy jog
- Stretch: quads, hamstrings, calves (30sec each)

---

**TYPE A: INTERVALS (speed work)**

Location: LC de Villiers track

| Week | Intervals | Target Pace per rep | Recovery |
|------|-----------|--------------------|---------| 
| 13-14 | 6 x 800m | 3:45-3:50 (4:41-4:48/km) | 2min easy jog |
| 15-16 | 8 x 800m | 3:40-3:45 | 90sec jog |
| 17-18 | 6 x 1km | 4:20-4:25 | 90sec jog |
| 19 | 8 x 800m | 3:35-3:40 | 90sec jog |

**What intervals feel like:** Hard but controlled. You should finish each rep feeling like you could've gone faster. If you're dying by rep 3, you went out too fast.

---

**TYPE B: TEMPO RUN (threshold work)**

| Week | Distance | Target Pace |
|------|----------|------------|
| 13-14 | 4km tempo | 5:15-5:25/km |
| 15-16 | 5km tempo | 5:10-5:20/km |
| 17-18 | 6km tempo | 5:05-5:15/km |

Warm-up 2km + tempo + cool-down 2km = ~10km total session.

**What tempo feels like:** "Comfortably hard." You could speak 3-4 words at a time. Breathing hard but controlled. Not gasping. Not a sprint. Sustained effort.

## **RACE WEEK PROTOCOLS:**

---

### **WEEK 20: AUGUST 23 - ABSA TSHWANE 10K**

**Goal: Sub-60min (6:00/km average)**

**Monday Aug 17:** 8km easy
**Wednesday Aug 19:** 4 x 1km @ 4:20 (controlled, not exhausting) + warm-up/cool-down
**Thursday Aug 20:** Gym - Nordic curls + core only, keep it light
**Friday Aug 21:** Complete REST
**Saturday Aug 22:** 2km very easy shakeout OR complete rest
**Sunday Aug 23:** 🔥 RACE DAY 🔥

**Race strategy:**
- Km 1-3: Run 6:10-6:15/km. Let everyone go. You will pass them later.
- Km 4-7: Settle into 5:55-6:00/km. Find a rhythm.
- Km 8-9: Start pushing. 5:45-5:50/km.
- Km 10: Whatever you have left. Empty the tank.
- If you've paced well, you'll negative split and finish strong.

---

### **WEEK 21 (SPLIT): SEPTEMBER 5 - BROOKLYN 10K**

**Goal: Sub-55min (5:30/km)**

**Monday:** 8km easy
**Wednesday Sept 2:** 6 x 800m @ 3:35 (sharp, not tired)
**Thursday Sept 3:** Gym - light, Nordic curls + core
**Friday Sept 4:** REST
**Saturday Sept 5:** 2km shakeout
**Sunday Sept 5:** 🔥 BROOKLYN 10K 🔥

**Race strategy:**
- More aggressive than August. You've raced once, you know what it feels like.
- Km 1-2: 5:35/km, controlled
- Km 3-7: 5:25-5:30/km sustained
- Km 8-10: Push through, aim for 5:20/km

---

### **TUKS NIGHT RACE 5K - SEPTEMBER 16:**

**Goal: Sub-25min (5:00/km)**

This race is mid-week (Wednesday evening). Treat the whole week lighter.

**Monday Sept 14:** 6km easy only
**Tuesday Sept 15:** REST
**Wednesday Sept 16:** 🔥 TUKS NIGHT RACE 🔥

**Race strategy:**
- Km 1: Don't go out faster than 4:55/km. Night race atmosphere will get you. Resist.
- Km 2-3: Find 5:00/km rhythm. Hold it.
- Km 4: Start hurting. That's correct. Keep form.
- Km 5: Everything left. Sprint finish.
- Sub-25 means you're ready for sub-2:00 Soweto.

**Thursday Sept 17:** REST (recovery from race)
**Sunday Sept 20:** 14km easy (back into it)

---

### **FORD 3-IN-1 HALF MARATHON - SEPTEMBER 26:**

**Goal: Sub-2:05 (5:55/km) - CONTROLLED DRESS REHEARSAL**

This is not a race you destroy yourself in. It is a test run for Soweto. Race everything the same: same breakfast, same shoes, same warm-up, same pacing strategy. Then adapt for Soweto.

**Race week:**
- **Monday Sept 21:** 6km easy
- **Wednesday Sept 23:** 4km easy + 4 x 100m strides
- **Thursday Sept 24:** REST
- **Friday Sept 25:** 3km shakeout + 4 x 100m strides
- **Saturday Sept 26:** 🎯 FORD HALF MARATHON 🎯

**Race strategy:**
- Km 1-5: 6:00-6:05/km. Deliberately conservative. Let people go.
- Km 6-12: 5:55/km. Controlled. Breathing easy. You should feel like you're holding back.
- Km 13-18: 5:50/km. Start working. This is where it gets real.
- Km 19-21: Assess. If you have legs, push. If not, maintain.
- **After:** Note everything. What worked, what didn't, what you'll change for Soweto.

## **5KM TIME TRIAL (End of Week 19):**

Before the race season starts, do this to know exactly where you stand:

- Warm-up: 2km easy + 4 strides
- Run 5km ALL OUT at LC de Villiers
- Cool-down: 2km easy

**Target: Sub-25:00 (5:00/km)**

If you hit sub-25min: Sub-60min 10K in August is very comfortable. Sub-2:00 Soweto is realistic.
If you run 25-27min: Sub-60min is still achievable. Sub-2:00 at Soweto will require a strong Phase 3.
If you run over 27min: Adjust expectations slightly. Soweto will be sub-2:10 rather than sub-2:00. Still a great result.
