**THE RACE PLAN:**

**Km 1-5: HOLD BACK (target 5:50-5:55/km)**

The hardest part of the race is not going out too fast. The atmosphere, the crowd, fresh legs - everything tells you to run faster. Don't. You will regret it at km 17 if you do.

Run 5:50-5:55/km for the first 5km. Let people go. You will see them again later.

**Km 6-12: SETTLE (target 5:41-5:45/km)**

Find your rhythm. This should feel controlled. Breathing hard but manageable. This is the pace you've practiced every Wednesday for months.

Drink water at every aid station. Small sips only.

**Km 13-16: WORK (target 5:41/km)**

The race gets real here. Your legs start feeling it. This is where your training kicks in - you've been here in long runs. You know this feeling.

Mental cue: "I've run 23km in training. I'm at km 14. I've done this before."

**Km 17-19: DIG (target 5:35-5:41/km)**

This is where character is built. It will hurt. Everyone hurts here. The question is who slows down and who holds on.

Cue: "One km at a time. Just get to the next marker."

**Km 20-21.1: EMPTY THE TANK**

You can see the finish. Everything you have left goes here. No saving anything.

The Soweto crowd at the finish is electric. Use it.

## **TAPER PHILOSOPHY:**

**What tapering is NOT:**
- ❌ "I haven't done enough - one more long run" → No. Trust the work.
- ❌ Complete rest for 3 weeks → No. Keep legs moving.
- ❌ Trying something new (food, gear, routine) → No. Nothing new.

**What tapering IS:**
- ✅ Volume drops, sharpness stays
- ✅ Legs feel almost too good (taper madness is real - don't act on it)
- ✅ Body is supercompensating - you're getting stronger by resting
- ✅ Sleep more than usual
- ✅ Hydrate consistently
- ✅ Trust 6+ months of consistent work
