# **6. PHASE 4: PEAK PHASE**

## **WEEKS 37-41 (OCTOBER 11 - NOVEMBER 8, 2026)**

**Final peak before taper - highest quality training**

### **PRIMARY GOALS:**

1. ✅ Race Ford Half Marathon as dress rehearsal (Week 38)
2. ✅ Final peak long runs (22-23km)
3. ✅ Prove race pace for extended distances (12-14km @ 5:15/km)
4. ✅ Sharpen speed (fast 800m repeats)
5. ✅ Build confidence: "I'm ready for Soweto"

**Volume:** 50-58km per week (slightly lower than Phase 3, but higher intensity)

---

### **WEEKLY STRUCTURE (WEEKS 37, 39-41):**

**Note: Week 38 is Ford Half Marathon race week**

---

### **MONDAY: FAST INTERVALS**

**Sharpening workouts - building speed while maintaining endurance**

**Weeks 37:**
- **10 x 800m @ 3:25-3:30** (90sec jog recovery)
- These should feel SHARP and fast
- But controlled, not all-out

**Weeks 39-40:**
- **5 x 1km @ 4:10-4:15** (90sec jog recovery)
- OR **12 x 400m @ 1:35-1:38** (60sec jog recovery)
- Alternate weeks to keep body adapting

**Week 41:**
- **8 x 800m @ 3:25** (90sec recovery)
- Last hard speed session before taper
- Should feel sharp, controlled, confident

**Warm-up:** 2km easy + drills + strides (always)

**Cool-down:** 2km easy + stretching (always)

**Evening:** Complete rest (no gym during peak phase)

---

### **TUESDAY: EASY RUN**

**8km conversational pace**

- Pace: 6:00-6:30/km
- Recovery from Monday
- Keep it comfortable

---

### **WEDNESDAY: RACE PACE TEMPO**

**🔥 CRITICAL WORKOUT - Proving Ground 🔥**

**Weeks 37:**
- 2km warm-up
- **10km @ 5:30/km** (conservative race pace - if targeting 1:55-2:00 finish)
- 2km cool-down

**Weeks 39-40:**
- 2km warm-up
- **12km @ 5:15/km** (EXACTLY goal race pace for 1:50-1:55)
- 2km cool-down
- **This is it - proving you can hold goal pace**

**Week 41:**
- 2km warm-up
- **14km @ 5:15/km** (extending race pace even further)
- 2km cool-down
- **If you can do this, you can CRUSH 21km @ 5:15/km**

**Mental Focus:**
- Lock into rhythm
- Practice race-day breathing
- Visualize Soweto finish line
- Build unshakeable confidence

**This workout is:**
- Physical proof your goal is realistic
- Mental preparation for race day
- Pacing practice
- Confidence builder

---

### **THURSDAY: EASY RUN OR REST**

**6km easy OR complete rest**

- Listen to body
- Peak training is demanding
- Rest when needed

---

### **FRIDAY: EASY RUN**

**6-8km easy**

- Prepare for Saturday long run
- Conversational pace

**Evening:**
- Mobility/stretching (30min)
- Foam rolling
- Hip openers

---

### **SATURDAY: LONG RUN**

**Weeks 37:**
- **20km** (first week back from Ford race)
  - First 14km easy (5:45-6:00/km)
  - Last 6km @ 5:15-5:30/km
  - Confidence builder

**Weeks 39-40:**
- **22-23km** (FINAL PEAK LONG RUNS)
  - First 16km easy (5:45-6:00/km)
  - Middle 4km @ 5:30/km
  - Last 2-3km @ 5:00-5:15/km (strong finish)
  - **Your longest run ever**
  - **Mental preparation: "If I can do 23km, 21km is manageable"**

**Week 41:**
- **18km** (starting to reduce volume)
  - First 12km easy
  - Last 6km @ 5:15/km (race pace)
  - Feeling good, not grinding

**Post-Long Run:**
- Full recovery protocol (see Phase 3)
- Ice bath if available
- Foam rolling + stretching
- Refuel properly
- Rest afternoon

**Afternoon:**
- Comprehensive recovery (45min)
- Nap if possible

---

### **SUNDAY: RECOVERY**

**Complete rest OR 5km very easy jog**

- Most weeks: complete rest
- Occasionally: very easy jog if feeling fresh
- Always: recovery walk option
- Meal prep for week
- Mental preparation for next week

---

### **WEEK 38: FORD 3-IN-1 HALF MARATHON (SEPTEMBER 26)**

**🎯 DRESS REHEARSAL - Most Important Training Day 🎯**

**This is your practice race. Everything you learn here applies to Soweto.**

**Monday:** Rest or 3km shakeout jog (very easy)

**Tuesday:** 6km easy

**Wednesday:** 4km easy + 4x100m strides (shake out legs)

**Thursday:** COMPLETE REST (hydrate, sleep, prepare)

**Friday:** 3km shakeout + 4x100m strides (loosen up)

**SATURDAY SEPTEMBER 26: RACE DAY - FORD HALF MARATHON** 🔥

**Goal: 1:58-2:03 (5:35-5:48/km average)**
**Effort: 80% - CONTROLLED, NOT ALL-OUT**

**This is practice, NOT the goal race**

**See detailed race strategy in Section 8**

**Sunday:** Recovery walk only (no running)

---

### **WEEK 39: POST-FORD RECOVERY WEEK**

**Volume: 35-40km (way down from normal)**

**Mon-Wed:** 
- Easy 5-6km runs or walks
- Just moving gently
- Let body recover from race

**Thu:** Rest

**Fri:** 6km easy

**Sat:** 14km long run (getting back into it)

**Sun:** Rest

**Purpose:**
- Full recovery from Ford race
- Absorb lessons learned
- Prepare for final 2-week peak
- Come back fresh and strong
