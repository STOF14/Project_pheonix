Pacing Strategy Guide

The Golden Rule: Negative Splits

Run the second half faster than the first half. This requires discipline to start SLOW.

Why it works:

  • Conserves energy for when you need it
  • Mentally easier (passing people vs getting passed)
  • Prevents blowing up at 15km
  • Proven strategy of elite runners
What TO Do
  • Start 10-15 sec/km SLOWER than goal pace
  • Let people pass you early - that's okay
  • Check watch every km, not every 100m
  • Take water at every station (even just a sip)
  • Focus on YOUR race, not others
  • Pick up pace after halfway point
  • Sprint the final 400m - leave nothing behind
What NOT To Do
  • Go out with the fast starters (you'll die)
  • Skip water stations ("I'll save time")
  • Race against random people around you
  • Panic if you're "behind pace" early
  • Wear new shoes or new kit on race day
  • Eat anything different from training
  • Stay up late the night before

INJURY PREVENTION

STAY HEALTHY, TRAIN CONSISTENTLY

Critical Principle

The best workout is the one you can do tomorrow. Injury prevention is MORE important than any single training session. Listen to your body and act immediately on warning signs.

Nordic Curls: Non-Negotiable

Your #1 Injury Prevention Exercise

Why: Your hamstrings are weak. This prevents hamstring strains, knee injuries, and IT band issues.

Frequency: 3-4x per week (Monday after runs, plus 2-3 other days)

How to Perform:

  1. Kneel on soft surface (mat, towel)
  2. Anchor feet under couch/bed OR have partner hold ankles
  3. Keep body straight from knees to shoulders
  4. Lower forward SLOWLY (5 seconds), controlling with hamstrings
  5. Use hands to catch yourself at bottom
  6. Push back to start with hands

Nordic Curls Progression

Week Sets x Reps Notes
1-4 3 x 3-4 Focus on 5sec eccentric, expect to be sore
5-8 3 x 6-7 Building strength, should feel easier
9-12 3 x 8-10 Strong hamstrings achieved

Common Runner Injuries

Injury Symptoms Prevention Treatment
Runner's Knee Pain around kneecap Strengthen quads and glutes, proper shoes Rest 3-5 days, ice, strengthen glutes
Shin Splints Pain along shin bone Gradual mileage increase, proper shoes Rest, ice, calf stretches, foam rolling
IT Band Syndrome Outside knee pain Foam roll IT band daily, strengthen hips Foam rolling, rest, hip strengthening
Plantar Fasciitis Heel pain (worst in morning) Calf stretches daily, proper arch support Stretch calves, roll foot on ball, ice, rest
Hamstring Strain Back of thigh pain Nordic curls 3-4x/week, proper warm-up STOP running immediately, ice, gentle stretch
Achilles Tendinitis Back of ankle pain Calf strength, gradual progression Eccentric calf raises, ice, rest

Recovery Protocols

Daily (10min)
Post-run stretching: hamstrings, quads, calves, hip flexors (30sec each)
3x per Week (20min)
Foam rolling: quads, hamstrings, calves, IT band, glutes
Weekly (30min)
Deep stretching session or yoga flow for runners
Post-Long Run
Ice bath (10min) OR contrast shower + comprehensive foam rolling
When to Take an Unplanned Rest Day
  • Sharp pain that doesn't improve with warm-up
  • Resting heart rate 10+ bpm higher than normal
  • Extreme fatigue (can't complete warm-up)
  • Illness (fever, flu, severe cold)
  • Persistent soreness that affects running form
  • Mental burnout (dreading every run)

Remember: One rest day now prevents two weeks off later. Always err on side of caution.

Your First 30 Days

ONLY ONE JOB: Post Every Single Day for 30 Days

Doesn't matter if it's "good" content. Consistency beats quality in the early days. The algorithm needs data, and you need the habit.

  • Daily TikTok/IG Reels post minimum (feed)
  • 5+ IG Stories daily (easier, more casual)
  • Reply to every comment, every day
  • Engage with 20+ accounts in running/fitness niche daily

By Day 30: You'll know what resonates, have an audience (even if small), and content creation will feel natural instead of forced.

Non-Negotiable Principles

Consistency > Perfection
Posting every day beats the perfect post once a week. The algorithm rewards consistency. Your audience wants regular updates.
Authenticity > Polish
Shaky phone footage of real struggle beats perfectly edited fake enthusiasm. Show bad runs, doubts, setbacks — that's what goes viral.
Story > Stats
"I struggled through 6km today" beats "6km at 6:15 pace". People connect with emotion and struggle, not numbers and statistics.
Value > Vanity
1,000 engaged followers beats 10,000 dead ones. Help people, share what you learn, build real relationships through comments.
