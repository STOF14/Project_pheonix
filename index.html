1. Nordic Curls — THE CRITICAL EXERCISE

You were a sprinter. Your hamstrings are both your greatest asset and your biggest injury risk. Nordic curls will keep you running when others get hurt.

Week Sets x Reps
1-2 3 x 4 (5sec lower, very controlled)
3-4 3 x 5
5-6 3 x 6
7-8 3 x 7
9-10 3 x 8
11-12 3 x 9

How to do them:

  • Kneel on a mat, anchor feet under a bench or have someone hold them
  • Slowly lower your body forward, controlling entirely with your hamstrings (5 seconds down)
  • Catch yourself with your hands at the bottom
  • Push back up to start position
  • Rest 90 seconds between sets
  • Never skip these

2. Single-Leg Romanian Deadlifts

  • Bodyweight or light dumbbell (5-10kg)
  • 3 sets x 8 reps per leg
  • Hinge at the hip, feel the hamstring stretch
  • Rest 60sec

3. Glute Bridges

  • 3 sets x 15 reps
  • Hold top 2 seconds, squeeze glutes hard
  • Rest 60sec

4. Plank

  • 3 sets x 30-40sec
  • Build to 50sec by Week 12
  • Rest 45sec

5. Dead Bugs

  • 3 sets x 10 reps (5 each side)
  • Keep your lower back flat on the floor the entire time

Optional if time allows:

  • Pull-ups or lat pulldown: 3 x 6-8
  • Push-ups: 3 x 12

Total gym time: 40-50min. Get in, work hard, get out.

Km 1-5: HOLD BACK (target 5:50-5:55/km)

The hardest part of the race is not going out too fast. The atmosphere, the crowd, fresh legs - everything tells you to run faster. Don't. You will regret it at km 17 if you do.

Run 5:50-5:55/km for the first 5km. Let people go. You will see them again later.

Km 6-12: SETTLE (target 5:41-5:45/km)

Find your rhythm. This should feel controlled. Breathing hard but manageable. This is the pace you've practiced every Wednesday for months.

Drink water at every aid station. Small sips only.

TAPER PHILOSOPHY:

What tapering is NOT:

  • ❌ "I haven't done enough - one more long run" → No. Trust the work.
  • ❌ Complete rest for 3 weeks → No. Keep legs moving.
  • ❌ Trying something new (food, gear, routine) → No. Nothing new.

What tapering IS:

  • ✅ Volume drops, sharpness stays
  • ✅ Legs feel almost too good (taper madness is real - don't act on it)
  • ✅ Body is supercompensating - you're getting stronger by resting
  • ✅ Sleep more than usual
  • ✅ Hydrate consistently
  • ✅ Trust 6+ months of consistent work

Runner's Nutrition Principles:

Pre-Run Eating:

Easy runs (Monday/Wednesday):

  • If running in the afternoon: normal lunch 2-3hrs before is fine
  • No special pre-run food needed
  • Just be hydrated

Wednesday hard workouts:

  • Proper lunch 2-3hrs before (rice + chicken works well)
  • Avoid heavy or greasy food on workout days
  • Banana 30-45min before if hungry

Sunday long run (critical - practice this for race day):

  • Light breakfast 60-90min before run
  • 2 slices toast + honey + banana, OR oats (50g) + banana + honey
  • 500ml water
  • Coffee if that's your normal routine
  • This is your race day breakfast. Practice it every Sunday.

Post-Run Recovery:

Within 30min of finishing any run:

  • Eat something: banana + protein source (eggs, chicken, milk)
  • This is when your body absorbs recovery nutrition most effectively
  • Don't skip this, especially after long runs and hard workouts

Hydration:

  • Daily minimum: 2.5-3L water
  • Training days: 3-3.5L (more in summer/hot weather)
  • Long run days: 3.5-4L
  • Urine color guide: pale yellow = hydrated | dark yellow = drink more
  • Bananas + consistent hydration = most of your recovery sorted

Weight Management:

Don't diet. Train consistently, eat real food, and the weight comes off naturally. A 3-4kg drop over Phase 1 is normal without doing anything special. Chasing weight aggressively while training hard leads to injury and fatigue.

If you want to nudge it: reduce portion sizes slightly on rest days (Friday/Saturday) when you're not burning as much.

Key Foods for Runners on This Budget:

Bananas - cheapest, most practical running food. Buy 8kg/month minimum. Pre-run fuel, post-run recovery, race day nutrition. Always have them.

Oats - best pre-run breakfast. Cheap, slow-releasing energy, easy to digest.

Eggs - best post-run recovery protein on this budget. Scrambled eggs on toast after a long run is a proper recovery meal.

Rice - primary carbohydrate. Eat more on training days, slightly less on rest days.

Chicken - your main protein. Batch cook on Sundays for the week. Saves time and money.

Honey - specifically for training. On toast pre-run, mixed into oats, on race day. Worth the R25-30.
