### **MONDAY: SPEED SESSION (Intervals)**

**Location:** LC de Villiers track

**Warm-up (15min):**
- 2km easy jog
- Dynamic drills:
  - Leg swings: 10 forward/back, 10 side/side each leg
  - High knees: 20m x 2
  - Butt kicks: 20m x 2
  - A-skips: 20m x 2
  - B-skips: 20m x 2
- 4 x 100m strides @ 70% effort
- 2min rest, prepare for workout

**Main Workout:**

**Weeks 14-15:**
- **6 x 800m @ 3:35-3:40** (4:29-4:36/km pace)
- Recovery: 2min jog between reps (400m easy)
- **This will feel HARD - you're not used to speed work**
- Focus: Even pacing (don't go out too fast on rep 1)
- Target splits: 3:35, 3:37, 3:38, 3:38, 3:37, 3:35

**Weeks 19-20:**
- **8 x 800m @ 3:30-3:35** (4:22-4:29/km pace)
- Recovery: 90sec jog between reps
- **Getting faster and more comfortable**
- Target: More even pacing across all 8 reps

**Cool-down (15min):**
- 2km easy jog
- Stretching: 10min focusing on quads, hamstrings, calves, hip flexors

**Total: 60-75min**

**Why this workout:**
- Builds speed endurance
- Teaches pace control
- Improves lactate threshold
- Maintains your sprint speed while building distance

**Evening:** 
- Complete rest (no gym)
- Let body recover from speed work
- Light stretching if needed
- Foam rolling optional

### **WEDNESDAY: TEMPO RUN**

**Warm-up:** 2km easy jog

**Main Workout:**

**Weeks 14-15:**
- **4km @ 5:00-5:15/km** (comfortably hard - "tempo" effort)
- Should feel: Challenging but sustainable
- Breathing: Can speak 3-5 words at a time
- This is "comfortably uncomfortable"
- Not gasping, but definitely working

**Weeks 19-20:**
- **6km @ 4:50-5:05/km**
- Getting closer to goal race pace
- Building confidence at faster paces

**Cool-down:** 2km easy jog

**Total: 8-10km**

**Why this workout:**
- Builds lactate threshold (ability to hold faster pace longer)
- Teaches "comfortably hard" effort (key for race day)
- Bridges gap between easy running and intervals
- Most important workout for half marathon success

**Evening (Optional):**
- Light upper body gym session (30min) IF feeling fresh
  - Pull-ups: 3x6-8
  - Push-ups: 3x12-15
  - Rows: 3x10
  - Core work: 10min

### **SATURDAY: LONG RUN**

**Progressive Long Run with Fast Finish**

**Weeks 14-15:** 16-17km
- First 12km @ 5:45-6:00/km (easy, conversational)
- Last 4-5km @ 5:15-5:30/km (pick up pace, "comfortably hard")
- Practice negative splitting (second half faster than first)
- Simulates race day pacing

**Weeks 19-20:** 18-20km
- First 13km easy (5:45-6:00/km)
- Last 5-7km faster (5:15-5:30/km)
- Building endurance + practicing strong finish

**Pre-Run:**
- Light breakfast 90-120min before
- 500ml water
- Bathroom
- Dynamic warm-up (5min)

**During Run:**
- Carry water for anything over 16km
- Can take small sips every 15-20min
- Don't chug - just wet mouth and throat

**Post-Run:**
- 5min walk
- 500ml water immediately
- Banana or snack within 30min
- Stretch: 10-15min

**Afternoon:**
- Foam rolling + stretching (30min)
- Nap if possible
- Elevate legs

### **GYM WORK (PHASE 2):**

**Frequency:** 1-2x per week (optional, based on fatigue)

**Focus:** Maintenance only

**Session Structure (30-40min):**

1. **Nordic Curls: 2-3 sets x 8-10 reps**
   - STILL critical for hamstrings
   - Never stop doing these
   - Your injury insurance

2. **Light Upper Body (if including):**
   - Pull-ups: 3x6-8
   - Push-ups: 3x12-15
   - Rows: 3x10
   - Shoulder press: 3x8-10

3. **Core Work:**
   - Planks: 3x30-45sec
   - Side planks: 3x20-30sec each
   - Dead bugs: 3x12
   - Pallof press: 3x10 each side

**When to do it:**
- Tuesday evening (if skipped Wednesday gym)
- Thursday evening (if feeling fresh)
- **NOT after Monday intervals or Saturday long run**

**Why less gym:**
- Running is now your primary training stimulus
- Gym is supplementary for injury prevention
- Recovery capacity is limited - prioritize running
- Strength maintenance vs building phase
