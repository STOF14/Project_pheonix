### **RACE STRATEGY:**

**Goal: Sub-60 minutes (6:00/km average pace)**

**But more importantly: Learn the race experience**

---

**KM 0-2: CONTROLLED START (6:10-6:20/km)**

**THIS IS THE HARDEST PART - GOING SLOW WHEN EVERYONE ELSE SURGES**

- Gun goes off, crowd surges forward
- **LET THEM GO**
- People will pass you - that's OK
- Start 10-20 seconds per km SLOWER than goal pace
- First km might be 6:20 because of crowd
- **This is smart racing, not being slow**

**Mental cues:**
- "I'm racing the last km, not the first km"
- "Slow and steady wins the race"
- "Those people passing me will blow up at km 8"

**What to expect:**
- Adrenaline is HIGH
- Everything feels easy (IT'S A LIE - you'll pay later if you go too fast)
- Breathing should feel comfortable
- If you're breathing hard at km 2: YOU WENT OUT TOO FAST, slow down now

---

**KM 2-5: SETTLE INTO RHYTHM (6:00-6:10/km)**

- Crowd has thinned out
- Find your space on the road
- Lock into 6:00-6:05/km pace
- Breathing: Steady, controlled, could still talk in short sentences
- Stay relaxed:
  - Shoulders down (check every km - they creep up)
  - Hands loose (don't clench fists)
  - Jaw relaxed
  - Breathing smooth

**Aid Station at ~km 3:**
- **Your first race aid station experience**
- Slow down slightly as you approach
- Grab water cup with outside hand (less reaching across)
- Options:
  - Walk through aid station while drinking (RECOMMENDED for first race)
  - OR slow to shuffle while drinking
  - OR skip it entirely (you don't need water for 10K)
- Drink a few sips, dump rest over head if hot
- Don't chug entire cup (stomach cramps)
- Resume pace smoothly

**Km 5 Check:**
- Time should be around 30:00-30:30
- How do you feel?
  - Breathing OK? Good.
  - Legs feel fresh? Good.
  - Stomach OK? Good.
- You're halfway done!

---

**KM 5-8: HOLD THE LINE (5:55-6:05/km)**

- **This is where the race really starts**
- Mental battle begins
- Legs start feeling it
- Brain says "slow down"
- **You say "NO - maintain pace"**

**Mental strategies:**
- Break it into chunks:
  - "Just get to km 6"
  - "Just get to km 7"
  - One kilometer at a time
- Count runners you pass (motivation)
- Focus on form:
  - Quick cadence (180 steps/min)
  - Arms driving forward/back (not side to side)
  - Breathing rhythm (2 steps in, 2 steps out)
- Positive self-talk:
  - "I've run 8km dozens of times in training"
  - "I'm strong"
  - "I can do this"

**Aid Station at ~km 7:**
- Same approach as km 3
- Small sips only
- Resume pace

**Things that might happen (normal):**
- Legs feel heavy - push through, it passes
- Side stitch - slow breathing, it passes
- "I want to quit" thoughts - ignore them, they lie
- Someone passes you - let them go, run YOUR race

---

**KM 8-9.5: THE GRIND (5:50-6:00/km)**

- **Hardest part of the race**
- Everything hurts
- Brain is screaming to stop
- **THIS IS WHERE YOU PROVE WHO YOU ARE**

**Mental warfare:**
- "I didn't train for 6 months to quit now"
- "Everyone hurts - the winners push through"
- "Less than 2km left - I can suffer for 12 more minutes"
- "This pain is temporary, the finish photo is forever"

**Physical:**
- Form is deteriorating - FIGHT TO MAINTAIN IT
  - Shoulders back
  - Head up (looking down makes you slower)
  - Quick cadence
  - Pump arms
- Pick a person ahead and hunt them down
- Every person you pass is a win

**If you need to:**
- Slow to 6:10-6:15/km to survive - that's OK
- Better to slow slightly than blow up completely
- But DON'T WALK (you'll lose too much time)

---

**KM 9.5-10: ALL-OUT SPRINT FINISH (however fast you can go)**

- You can see the finish line OR hear the announcer
- **THIS IS IT - EVERYTHING YOU HAVE LEFT**
- Your sub-11 100m sprint speed shows up here
- Empty the tank completely

**Sprint checklist:**
- Head up, eyes on finish line
- Arms pumping hard
- Quick turnover
- Ignore the pain
- **SPRINT LIKE YOU'RE RACING FOR GOLD**

**Finish line approach:**
- Big smile for the camera (someone's taking your photo)
- Hands in the air (celebrate!)
- Cross the line with authority

**Finish time: Hopefully sub-60:00!**

---

### **Immediately After Finishing:**

**First 60 seconds:**
- **KEEP WALKING** (don't stop suddenly or sit down)
- Hands on head (opens airways, easier breathing)
- Breathe deeply
- Accept your medal
- Collect your race goodie bag (if provided)

**First 5 minutes:**
- Keep walking slowly
- Find shade if hot
- Drink water (sips, not chugs)
- Banana if they're handing out (eat it)
- Call/text someone: "I JUST FINISHED MY FIRST RACE!"

**First 30 minutes:**
- Gentle stretching (hamstrings, quads, calves - 30sec each)
- More water (aim for 500ml in first 30min)
- Find your friends/family
- Take photos with medal
- **CELEBRATE - YOU JUST FINISHED YOUR FIRST RACE!**

**First 2 hours:**
- Change into dry clothes
- Eat real food within 90min:
  - Protein + carbs (chicken wrap, burger, pasta, whatever sounds good)
  - Chocolate milk if available (perfect recovery drink)
- More water
- Get home, shower, rest

**Rest of day:**
- Elevate legs for 30min
- Ice bath or cold shower if available (10min)
- Light walking around (don't just sit all day)
- Eat well (protein + carbs)
- Hydrate (3-4L water throughout day)
- Reflect on the race:
  - What went well?
  - What would you do differently?
  - How did it feel?
  - What did you learn?

**Evening:**
- Light stretching (20min)
- Foam rolling (gentle, 15min)
- Early to bed (body needs recovery)
- Sleep well - you earned it!
